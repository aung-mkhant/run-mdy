The Beauty of Mandalay

Mandalay — Myanmar’s last royal capital — is a compact city of gilded monasteries, traditional crafts, and riverside life. Walk through bustling markets, meet local artisans, and discover the city’s layered history.

Shwe Nan Daw (Shwenandaw) is renowned for its extraordinary teak carvings and intimate temple architecture. Once part of the royal palace complex, its intricately carved interiors showcase masterful Burmese woodwork and devotional scenes.

U Bein Bridge — the world’s longest teak footbridge — spans Taungthaman Lake and offers unforgettable sunrises and sunsets. The weathered planks and stilted posts frame striking silhouettes and everyday life on the water.

Near Mandalay Hill, the twin lion guardians mark paths to temple viewpoints. These stone lions and the hilltop terraces provide panoramic vistas across the city and a calming place for sunset reflections. Read more
